## 5 Common Overcharging Patterns We Found

### 1. "Per Square Meter" Inflation (Found in 40% of quotes)
Contractors quote ₪X per square meter, but the square meter count is inflated by 10-30%. If your kitchen is 12 sqm, the quote says 15 sqm.

**How to catch it:** Measure your room yourself. Use ShiputzAI's floorplan tool to get AI-measured dimensions from a photo.

### 2. Premium Material Markup (Found in 35% of quotes)
The quote specifies "premium tiles" or "high-quality paint" without brand names or model numbers. The actual materials used are mid-range, but priced as premium.

**How to catch it:** Demand specific brand names and model numbers for every material. Then price-check them yourself.

### 3. Hidden "Preparation" Costs (Found in 60% of quotes)
"Surface preparation: ₪3,000" or "demolition and cleanup: ₪5,000" — these vague line items often cover work that should be included in the base price.

**How to catch it:** Ask exactly what "preparation" includes. Get a detailed breakdown.

### 4. Duplicate Line Items (Found in 20% of quotes)
The same work appears under different names. "Electrical installation" and "power point setup" might overlap significantly.

**How to catch it:** Map every line item to a specific physical area of work. If two items refer to the same wall or room, question it.

### 5. VAT Tricks (Found in 15% of quotes)
Some quotes show prices without VAT, making them look cheaper. Others add VAT twice or apply it to items that shouldn't have it (like the contractor's own labor in some cases).

**How to catch it:** Always confirm: is this price inclusive or exclusive of VAT? Get a clear total.
